1. Why We Exist
Every year, millions of students in tier-2 and tier-3 Indian cities complete engineering degrees without the one
thing they actually came for: a job.
It's not their fault. They are taught by instructors who haven't worked in industry. They learn from curricula written
fifteen years ago. They graduate into a "placement cell" that is a single faculty member with a WhatsApp group,
doing their best against impossible odds.
The system has failed them structurally. And the tragedy is compounded by the fact that India's tech industry is
simultaneously facing a talent shortage — because the demand is for Python developers, ML engineers, and
data scientists, not Java programmers who last updated their skills in 2014.
KodetoCareer exists to close this gap. Not by replacing colleges. Not by being another Udemy. By becoming the
infrastructure layer that connects real-world industry training to real employment outcomes — at scale, across
hundreds of colleges, for hundreds of thousands of students.
We are building the operating system for career-focused technical education in India.
---
2. Our Mission and Vision
Mission: To make quality technical training and guaranteed placement infrastructure accessible to every student
in India, regardless of which college they attend.
Vision (10-year): KodetoCareer is the trusted hiring pipeline for Indian tech employers and the definitive
learning-to-employment platform for technical graduates — the platform that produced the engineers who built
India's next generation of technology companies.

These are not marketing statements. These are operating beliefs that shape every product, hiring, and business
decision.
We believe placement is the product, not the feature.
Every company that sells "ed-tech" in India has eventually discovered that the only thing students — and their
parents — actually care about is: did you get me a job? We build everything backwards from that question.
We believe the training partner is the most underserved actor in education.
Colleges want outcomes. Students want jobs. Recruiters want talent. But nobody has built for the company in the
middle that does the actual hard work of upskilling and placing students. That's us.
We believe data compounds.
Every student we train generates data. Every placement we make generates trust. Every college we partner with
generates reach. These aren't linear — they compound. The platform we're building is designed to generate
compounding advantages that get stronger with every cohort.
We believe software should feel like a privilege, not an obligation.
Too many enterprise software products are tolerated, not loved. We want students to actually open the app every
day — not because they're forced to, but because it shows them their progress, helps them prepare, and
connects them to opportunity. That requires building software that respects their time and intelligence.
We believe trust is the foundation.
We make a placement promise. If we don't deliver, we lose. This means every claim we make must be
measurable and true. Our data cannot be massaged. Our certificates must be earned, not distributed. Our
placement numbers must be real.

5. Go-to-Market Strategy
Phase 1: Proof of Concept (Months 1–3)
Goal: 3 pilot colleges live on the platform with real students.
Tactic: Use existing relationships. The founder already has agreements with colleges. The MVP launch is not a
sales motion — it is a proof-of-concept deployment with partners who trust us.
Success metric: 500 active students, 80% daily engagement, 90% certificate delivery rate, 3 documented
placements.
Phase 2: Reference Build (Months 4–9)
Goal: Turn 3 pilot colleges into 3 case studies that sell for us.
Tactic:
 Document placement outcomes with verifiable data
 
 Produce 3 one-page case studies: College name, students trained, placement rate, average package
 
 Use these as the primary sales asset for new college acquisition
 
New college acquisition:
 Direct outreach to placement officers and college directors in target geography
 
 Attend education expos (EduTech India, regional events)
 
 LinkedIn targeted outreach to TPO (Training & Placement Officers)
 
Target: 10 colleges, 2,000 students
Phase 3: Scale (Year 2)
Goal: 50 colleges, 10,000 students, platform generates independent revenue.
Tactic:
 Hire 2 business development executives for college acquisition
 
 Partner with NASSCOM, CII, or AICTE for credibility and lead access
 
 Launch recruiter access model — turn placement data into a two-sided marketplace
 
 Begin SaaS licensing conversations with other training companies

6. Competitive Moat
Our competitive advantage is not any single feature. It is the combination of:
Data Moat: We will know more about student skills, learning patterns, and placement outcomes than any other
platform in our geography. This data has compounding value.
Relationship Moat: Colleges sign multi-year agreements. Students graduate and recommend us to their
colleges. Recruiters who hire through us come back.
Verification Moat: KodetoCareer certificates have a public verification URL. Over time, recruiters will come to
trust our certification because they have hired students who held them.
Operations Moat: Our platform makes the training partner's operations 10x more efficient. Switching away
means losing all their historical data, analytics, and workflows. High switching cost = long retention.

7. Culture & Values
7.1 How We Work
Ownership Culture:
Every team member owns their domain. A developer who builds the quiz system owns the quiz system — from
design review to production bugs. We don't pass problems around. We take them.
Bias to Shipping:
A feature that is 80% perfect and deployed is worth more than a feature that is 100% perfect in development. We
ship, we learn, we iterate. We do not plan in the dark for months.
Written Communication:
Decisions must be documented. The Founder Bible, PRDs, and architecture decisions exist so that the team can
move fast without constant synchronous coordination. If a decision isn't written down, it doesn't exist.
Data Over Opinions:
We don't argue about what students want. We measure it. If engagement on a feature is below 20%, the feature
is failing regardless of how strongly someone believes in it.

7.2 Hiring Principles
Hire for slope, not intercept.
We prefer a candidate who has improved dramatically over 12 months over a candidate who peaked early.
Learning speed matters more than current level.
Hire people who've shipped things.
Real projects, side products, open source contributions — evidence of delivery. We do not hire people whose
only experience is in academic settings.
Every hire must raise the team's average.
The standard for our 10th hire is higher than the standard for our 5th hire. We always ask: is this person better
than our current weakest link?
No brilliant jerks.
Technical excellence does not excuse poor collaboration, communication, or treatment of teammates. Culture is
non-negotiable.
7.3 Decision Making
For reversible decisions: individual or small group, move fast.
For irreversible decisions (architecture, pricing, key hires): write a one-pager, get input from all affected parties,
then decide.
The founder makes the final call on product direction, hiring, and business model. Engineering leads make final
calls on architecture. Design leads make final calls on UX within agreed frameworks.

The platform is the product and the business simultaneously. Every hour spent on manual work that could be

10. What We Will Not Do
Being clear about what we will not do is as important as knowing what we will.
We will not become a consumer ed-tech product. We serve the training partner model. Going B2C at scale
requires marketing budgets we do not have and will not chase.
We will not compromise on placement data integrity. Every placement number we publish must be real and
verifiable. A platform built on inflated numbers will collapse.

We will not build features for the sake of features. The platform will never have a feature that doesn't solve a

We will not raise VC money before we have product-market fit. We will build profitably or on founder capital

We will not abandon tier-2 colleges for metro colleges. Our mission is to serve the underserved. The moment
