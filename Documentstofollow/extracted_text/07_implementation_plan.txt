1. Team Structure
Recommended Team for MVP
Role | Count | Responsibilities
Backend Developer (Node.js) | 1–2 | API development, database, integrations
Frontend Developer (React.js) | 1 | Admin panel and college portal
Flutter Developer | 1 | Mobile app (iOS + Android)
UI/UX Designer | 1 | Figma designs, prototypes, handoff
DevOps / Infrastructure | 0.5 (part-time or backend dev doubles) | AWS setup, CI/CD, deployment
Product Manager / Founder | 1 | Requirement clarification, QA sign-off
QA Tester | 1 (from Week 6) | Test execution, bug reporting
Total: 5–7 people
If budget is constrained:
 A senior full-stack developer can handle both Node.js backend and React frontend
 
 Flutter developer is a separate skill set — do not compromise here
 
 UI/UX can be done by founder using the detailed brief and Figma templates initially

4. Risk Register
Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation
Video processing pipeline delayed | Medium | High | Build with YouTube embed first, own upload in Sprint 4
Flutter developer bottleneck | Medium | High | Prepare detailed Flutter widget specs before Sprint 1
College onboarding slower than expected | High | Medium | Don't wait for all 3 — go live with 1 college for alpha
MediaConvert costs exceed budget | Low | Medium | Cap video uploads at 720p initially, monitor spend
Push notifications not delivered on budget Android | Medium | Medium | Test FCM early (Sprint 1), have SMS
fallback
AWS RDS pricing | Low | Low | Start with t3.micro, upgrade on need
College admin resistance to adoption | Medium | High | Design simplest possible college portal for MVP
